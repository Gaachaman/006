Nature

Niigata is located in the Echigo Plain and is fed by the Shinano River, the longest river in Japan. The river basin has low marshy plains and numerous lagoons, and the coastline has the Niigata Coast and sand dunes known as the Niigata Dunes. In Niigata, where the four seasons change clearly, visitors can enjoy the rich nature of the mountains and the sea, as well as the tranquil countryside.

Food

Niigata offers an abundance of fresh seafood and rice, as well as Echigo soy sauce and locally brewed sake.

History And Culture

Niigata has a long historical heritage and is dotted with castle ruins, shrines and temples. Visitors can also enjoy traditional festivals and dances.

Sports

Niigata offers a wide variety of outdoor activities, from skiing and snowboarding in winter to hiking and water sports in summer. Many Niigata residents are also passionate about the local professional soccer team, Albirex. At home games, the entire city is dyed orange, the team's color.
